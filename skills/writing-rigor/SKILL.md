# Writing Rigor

Make the artifact useful to its audience, not merely polished.

## Fleet context

This skill is part of `ultracode-high`. For the full orchestration map, tool
catalog, agent roster, and card schemas, read
`../ultracode-high/references/runtime-context.md` when available. Writing edits
must preserve evidence, caveats, and actionability for the final integrator.

## Checks

- Audience and decision context are clear.
- First section states the thesis or bottom line.
- Structure supports scanning and action.
- Claims are sourced, calculated, or labeled as assumptions.
- Terminology is consistent.
- Language is concise and concrete.
- Risks and tradeoffs are not buried.
- Next actions are explicit when the artifact asks for action.
- Long artifacts have an executive summary or TLDR table when useful.

Do not smooth away uncertainty that matters to the reader.
